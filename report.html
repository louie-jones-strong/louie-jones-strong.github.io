Introduction

Introduction: What is the essential story being told by your site and what type of structure did you choose to implement?

For university I had to make a website as part of my web development module. At the same time I was wanting a portfolio anyway to show my work and accomplishments. So I decided to make a portfolio for this assignment.

My high level goals for this website are to provide a quickly consumable way for people to see if my skills match their needs and be able to contact me.

I then broke down this high level vision into sub-goals:

  • Provide ways for appropriate people to contact me
  • Look both technical and creative
  • Show off my skills
  • Be quickly consumable at a high level
  • Be easily expandable beyond the assignment, and when I create more work
  • Have search engine optimisation
  • Easily shareable
  • Learn new skills

As this was my first attempt at creating a full website, I embraced the agile methodology and implemented google analytics to give me feedback to continually improve it.

Inspiration

• Inspiration: State 3 things that have inspired you when creating your website (e.g. guest speakers, websites, artists, blogs).

Before starting on the design I researched current UI design trends:

  1. Skeuomorphism

    Skeuomorphism is meant to look realistic but needs a large amount of detailed art.

  2. Flat Design

    Flat Design is very simplistic focused on ease of creation and readability. This didn't meet my goal of making the site look creative or impressive.

  3. Neumorphism

    Neumorphism is a lower contrast monochromatic style that uses light and shadows to define edges of shapes. However, this leads to users struggling to recognise what they can interact with.

  4. Glassmorphism

    Glassmorphism was the newest design trend I found when planning. This has less constraints than neumorphism allowing me to define higher contrast to make the website more accessible.

I ended up deciding on using glassmorphism for this website due to it's simplicity combined with the modern look. However, one constraint of glassmorphism is that it requires the backgrounds to have well defined edges to highlight the blur effect from frosted glass.

I also took inspiration from the following:

  • takeboost.com
  • findmatthew.com
  • prashantsani.com

I also took a lot of inspiration from some of the websites that I also took code from, as listed in the resources section.

Accessibility

• Accessibility: State 3 ways in which your site is accessible to those with different abilities and needs (for example those with visual impairment)

While planning my portfolio, making the website accessible to all users was an important concern.

  • Choice of appropriate design style
  • I have chosen to use Roboto for the font as this is known as being accessible
  • Light and Dark colour themes
  • Scalable font, but defaulting to a large size where appropriate
  • Check contrast of text
  • Check that the entire website can be accessed without a mouse or touch, for example using the TAB key.
  • Provided alt text for all images.

Evaluation

I am very happy with the website I have made, especially given the limit understanding of html, css and JavaScript I had at the start of this module.

What Went Well

• Evaluation I: What aspects of your work do you think were particularly successful? Why?

I am proud of the design of the website I feel it has met my goals set out in the introduction. I am surprised with the amount of content I was able to produce in a small amount of time. Based on this I feel I have successfully managed the scope and time of this project. I've learnt how to make a responsive website that accommodates a diverse set of users with differing accessibility needs. To speed up my workflow for this website and future websites I created a tool in python that resizes all images and videos in a directory structure.

What Could have Gone Better

• Evaluation II: What aspects of your work could be improved? How might you do things differently another time?

One large mistake I made early on was developing the website on a 4k monitor, this meant that it didn't look like how I expected at lower screen resolutions. I ended up having to redo a lot of the html and css to work at lower resolutions. When making my next website I would continually test each change on each aspect ratio. I would have liked more time to be able to impliment more content, such as my work experience and group projects.However I have designed this website to be easily editable and I am planning to add tese projects along with any future ones to this portfolio soon. I should've asked for feedback on my initial plans before creating the webpage as this would've saved me a lot of time. I should've spent more time optimising loading times especially for the 24 hour blender challenges.
